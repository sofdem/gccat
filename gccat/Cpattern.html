Automaton

Taking advantage that all k-patterns have the same length k, it is straightforward to construct an automaton that only accepts solutions of the ğš™ğšŠğšğšğšğš›ğš— constraint. FigureÂ 5.317.2 depicts the automaton associated with the ğš™ğšŠğšğšğšğš›ğš— constraint of the Example slot. The construction can be done in three steps:

  • First, build a prefix tree of all the k-patterns. In the context of our example, this gives all arcs of FigureÂ 5.317.2, except self loops and the arc from s 3 to s 7 .

  • Second, find out the transitions that exit a leave of the tree. For this purpose we remove the first symbol of the corresponding k-pattern, add at the end of the remaining k-pattern a symbol corresponding to a stretch value, and check whether the new pattern belongs or not to the set of k-patterns of the ğš™ğšŠğšğšğšğš›ğš— constraint. When the new pattern belongs to the set of k-patterns we add a corresponding transition. For instance, in the context of our example, consider the leave s 3 that is associated with the 3-pattern 1,2,1. We remove the first symbol 1 and get 2,1.
